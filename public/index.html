Projects

UWAFT Accessibility Chair Lift - Cadillac Lyriq

Goal: Redesign the accessibility chair lift, originally limited to lifting 25 kg, to improve positioning, usability, and ease of assembly/removal.

Tools Used: NX, ANSYS (FEA Analysis)

Challenges:

  • Designing the lift to handle up to 300 lbs while keeping it compact for minimal space usage.
  • Ensuring the device avoids all airbag deployment zones in the vehicle.
  • Reducing friction losses while maintaining strength and reliability.
  • Balancing load capacity with ease of removal and installation.

Results / Learnings:

  • Successfully increased capacity to meet the 300 lb requirement without interfering with vehicle safety zones.
  • NX modeling enabled compact packaging for better fit inside the Cadillac Lyriq.
  • Trade-offs highlighted between strength and usability; modular connections improved assembly efficiency.
  • Gained experience in designing for real-world constraints including space, safety, and friction minimization.

Aluminum Air Galvanic Generator Testing Cell Redesign - Alumapower

Goal: Redesign and standardize the testing cell to be compatible with varying anode sizes (8mm, 14mm, and 28mm). Previous design worked only for 8mm thickness anodes.

Tools Used: Autodesk Inventor, Laser Cutting, 3D Printing

Challenges:

  • Anode and membrane needed to be as close as possible without causing short circuits or restricting airflow, to reduce internal resistance and maximize power generation.
  • Predetermined components and low budget limited implementation of mechanical improvements.

Results / Learnings:

  • Specified mechanical springs for each anode size to ensure efficient testing.
  • Redesigned mechanical components to streamline the assembly process.
  • Future improvements: develop a wider range of robust designs prior to procurement and testing to streamline the process.

Aluminum Air Galvanic Generator Motor Controller Enclosure - Alumapower

Goal: Design an enclosure to mount the motor controller that allows for quick removal and replacement.

Tools Used: Autodesk Inventor, 3D Printing

Challenges:

  • Minimizing size and weight caused thin corners, resulting in cracking during tapping.
  • Required 100% infill for tapping; balancing size vs. structural integrity required careful print setting adjustments.
  • Thin base caused warping during printing.

Results / Learnings:

  • Successfully optimized enclosure design to prevent printing defects while meeting size and accessibility requirements.
  • Key lesson: ensure sufficient infill and wall thickness for tapped parts.

APAR Mechanical Gripper

Goal: Design a gripper capable of handling a wide range of objects and lifting at least 3 kg without causing damage.

Tools Used: SolidWorks, 3D Printing

Challenges:

  • Budget of $50 limited servo options, forcing use of compact servos with lower torque.
  • Larger housing required for servos increased moment arm, amplifying torque demand and reducing lifting reliability.

Results / Learnings:

  • Prototype validated concept but did not consistently meet 3 kg lift requirement.
  • Key learnings: prioritize torque-to-size ratio, minimize moment arms, and incorporate compliant surfaces to prevent damage.

Hyperloop Chassis Brace - Waterloop

Goal / Requirements:

  • Design LIM teeth as the structural base for copper wire winding of hyperloop magnet coils.
  • Ensure the design maximizes energy efficiency by minimizing magnetic field losses.
  • Optimize the shape to fit the hyperloop body while allowing maximum copper coil orientation.

Tools Used: SolidWorks, ANSYS (FEA Analysis)

Challenges:

  • Fitting the LIM teeth within the limited space of the hyperloop body while maintaining optimal coil orientation.
  • Reducing energy losses (Eddy Currents) while keeping the design manufacturable and compact.

Solution / Learnings:

  • Applied laminated construction to the LIM teeth, significantly reducing Eddy Current losses and improving overall energy efficiency.
  • Validated the design using FEA to ensure structural integrity and performance under operational magnetic loads.
  • Learned the importance of early-stage concept verification to identify potential inefficiencies before full-scale production.

Hyperloop Linear Induction Motor - Waterloop

Goal / Requirements:

  • Design support bracing to connect the top and bottom sections of the hyperloop chassis.
  • Bracing must safely withstand the load of the lower half of the hyperloop, approximately 50 kg.
  • Ensure the design is modular, manufacturable, and compatible with operational constraints.

Tools Used: SolidWorks, ANSYS (FEA Analysis)

Challenges:

  • Initial design was prone to stress concentration points that required reinforcement.
  • 3D printed prototypes lacked sufficient material adhesion and structural strength to support the load.
  • Maintaining a compact design while providing adequate structural support presented trade-offs between size and strength.

Solution / Learnings:

  • Switched to sheet metal fabrication to ensure sufficient strength and durability under load.
  • Used FEA simulations to identify and reinforce stress-prone areas.
  • Learned to systematically evaluate material options early in the design process, weighing pros and cons to determine optimal solutions.
  • Gained insights into balancing manufacturability, load-bearing capacity, and design compactness for modular structural components.
